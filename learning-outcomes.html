Customer Insights Amid Disruption
Learning Outcomes & Module Structure

Course Overview

Every organization can benefit from a better understanding of customers. In Customer Insights Amid Disruption, students will learn the links between customers, marketing strategy, and performance, all within the context of digital and strategic disruption.
Course Focus:

This course prepares students to navigate the rapidly evolving marketing landscape where traditional rules no longer apply. Through case studies, business simulation, and hands-on analysis, students develop both strategic thinking and practical skills needed for modern marketing leadership.

Learning Outcome Measurability Standards:

All learning outcomes in this course use specific, observable, and measurable action verbs aligned with Bloom's Taxonomy. Students will demonstrate achievement through concrete deliverables, calculations, presentations, analyses, and other tangible evidence of learning.

Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs)

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

CLO 1: Digital Disruption Strategy
Develop comprehensive marketing strategies that leverage digital disruption opportunities, integrating Big Data insights across multiple channels (retail, digital, B2B, B2C) while demonstrating feasibility within budget and timeline constraints.
CLO 2: Customer Intelligence & Analytics
Apply advanced data analytics, including AI and machine learning techniques, to extract actionable customer insights and validate marketing decisions through quantitative analysis and ROI evaluation.
CLO 3: Executive Communication & Presentation
Create and deliver executive-level marketing presentations that synthesize complex digital data into clear, actionable recommendations within time constraints, demonstrating professional stakeholder management.
CLO 4: Customer-Centric Innovation
Design and implement customer-centric business models and go-to-market strategies for disruptive innovations, utilizing consumer behavior research and digital content strategies to shift customer behavior.
CLO 5: Adaptive Marketing Leadership
Navigate ambiguous marketing challenges in rapidly evolving digital ecosystems by demonstrating strategic pivoting, managing digital transformation initiatives, and leading customer-centric organizational change.

Module Learning Outcomes (MLOs)

Module 1: Big Data Disruption & Strategic Opportunities (Enables CLO 1, CLO 2)
  • MLO 1.1: Calculate and defend the strategic value of data assets using ROI frameworks and competitive benchmarking
  • MLO 1.2: Identify and prioritize three specific marketing opportunities created by digital disruption and Big Data availability
  • MLO 1.3: Design agile marketing campaign methodologies with measurable success metrics for consumer goods markets
Module 2: Digital Transformation & Consumer Behavior (Enables CLO 1, CLO 4)
  • MLO 2.1: Create behavioral change campaigns using targeted digital content and social psychology principles
  • MLO 2.2: Build consumer belief models using psychographic segmentation and emotional triggers
  • MLO 2.3: Evaluate disruption potential of emerging business models in traditional industries
Module 3: Digital Content Strategy & Customer Journey (Enables CLO 1, CLO 4)
  • MLO 3.1: Select and justify optimal content formats (video, interactive, social) for specific marketing objectives with performance justification
  • MLO 3.2: Translate customer data insights into actionable digital marketing leadership decisions with measurable outcomes
  • MLO 3.3: Map integrated content strategies across critical customer journey touchpoints from awareness to retention
Module 4: Customer Intelligence & Data Analytics (Enables CLO 2, CLO 4)
  • MLO 4.1: Execute conjoint analysis, A/B testing, and advanced quantitative research methodologies for market insights
  • MLO 4.2: Distinguish raw data from strategic insights and transform analytical findings into actionable marketing recommendations
  • MLO 4.3: Design CRM integration strategies that demonstrably improve customer lifetime value and retention metrics
Module 5: Go-to-Market Strategies & AI Innovation (Enables CLO 1, CLO 2, CLO 5)
  • MLO 5.1: Optimize launch strategies for disruptive technology startups using lean methodology and market validation techniques
  • MLO 5.2: Assess ethical AI applications in marketing while evaluating their impact on customer privacy and personalization
  • MLO 5.3: Construct customer-centric business models that leverage machine learning for predictive marketing and automated personalization
Module 6: Bringing It All Together - Transformation in the Face of Disruption (Enables CLO 1, CLO 5)
  • MLO 6.1: Compare traditional versus platform-based B2B business models, quantifying structural changes and competitive implications
  • MLO 6.2: Predict how digital intermediaries transform market curator roles using specific industry case studies
  • MLO 6.3: Forecast power shifts between brands, retailers, and consumers in omnichannel B2C markets using evidence-based scenario analysis
Module 7: Assessment, Reflection & Performance Analysis (Enables all CLOs)
  • MLO 7.1: Apply customer-centric strategies through multi-round business simulation while measuring performance against key metrics
  • MLO 7.2: Synthesize digital disruption concepts into comprehensive marketing strategies that address real-world complexity
  • MLO 7.3: Reflect critically on personal transformation from product-centric to customer-centric marketing mindset with evidence of growth
Module 8: Executive Communication & Live Application (Enables CLO 3)
  • MLO 8.1: Structure executive presentations using proven frameworks that synthesize complex marketing data into clear strategic narratives
  • MLO 8.2: Craft compelling elevator pitch recommendations that combine analytical rigor with persuasive storytelling
  • MLO 8.3: Deliver polished 10-minute team presentations on cutting-edge marketing research with professional Q&A handling

Assessment Strategy & Alignment

Assessment Philosophy:

This course employs a comprehensive assessment strategy that combines formative and summative evaluations to measure all learning outcomes. Students receive ongoing feedback through multiple touchpoints while building toward major deliverables that demonstrate integrated learning.

Summative Assessments

Customer Centricity Simulation with Disruptions (15%) MLOs 1.2, 4.3, 5.3, 6.1, 7.1, 7.3
Individual Case Analysis - Marlow with Limited Data (20%) MLOs 2.1, 4.1, 4.2, 5.1, 8.1, 8.2
Team Research Presentation with Pivot Challenge (25%) MLOs 5.2, 6.2, 6.3, 8.1, 8.3, 7.2
Online & In-Class Participation with Ambiguity Exercises (25%) MLOs 1.1, 2.3, 3.1, 3.2
Digital Strategy Portfolio with Mid-Course Pivot (15%) MLOs 1.3, 2.2, 3.3, 5.2, 7.2

Formative Assessments

Module Reflection Quizzes (Ungraded) All MLOs - Self-assessment & feedback
Peer Review Activities (Embedded) MLOs 2.2, 3.3, 7.2, 7.3
Discussion Forum Contributions (Embedded) MLOs 1.3, 5.2, 6.1, 6.2

Assessment Schedule & Methods

Assessment Timeline:
  • Week 2: Digital Strategy Portfolio - Part 1 (Consumer segmentation model)
  • Week 4: Individual Case Analysis - Marlow due (with intentionally limited/conflicting data provided)
  • Weeks 1-8: Customer Centricity Simulation (9 rounds + unexpected market disruptions in rounds 4 & 7)
  • Week 6: Digital Strategy Portfolio - PIVOT REQUIRED: Market disruption forces strategy revision
  • Week 7: Team Research Presentations - Last-minute data contradicts initial research (24hr pivot challenge)
  • Week 8: Digital Strategy Portfolio - Final integration with pivot reflection due
  • Ongoing: Participation with structured ambiguity exercises
CLO 5 (Adaptive Marketing Leadership) Assessment Methods:
  • Simulation Disruptions: Unexpected competitor moves, market crashes, regulatory changes mid-game
  • Limited Data Case Analysis: Students receive incomplete/contradictory information (like real executives)
  • Pivot Challenge: Week 6 portfolio requires complete strategy revision based on new market reality
  • 24-Hour Presentation Pivot: Teams get conflicting data day before presentation, must adapt
  • Ambiguity Reflection: Portfolio must include specific examples of decision-making under uncertainty
CLO 5 Evaluation Rubric:
  • Tolerance for Uncertainty: Proceeds with decisions despite incomplete information
  • Strategic Pivoting: Adapts approach when initial strategy proves ineffective
  • Stakeholder Communication: Appropriately communicates uncertainty without losing credibility
  • Learning Agility: Quickly incorporates new information and changes course
  • Process Documentation: Can articulate decision-making process under ambiguous conditions
Assessment Methods Variety:
  • Applied Learning: Business simulation with disruptions, case analysis with limited data
  • Adaptive Thinking: Mid-course pivots, real-time strategy adjustments
  • Communication: Executive presentations, pivot explanations, uncertainty management
  • Collaboration: Team crisis management, peer consultation under pressure
  • Reflection: Process documentation, ambiguity navigation analysis
  • Participation: Structured ambiguity exercises, rapid decision-making scenarios

Grading Criteria & Performance Standards

Performance Levels:

All assessments are evaluated using the following performance standards, aligned with each CLO:

Exemplary (90-100%)
Demonstrates mastery of digital disruption strategies with innovative integration of Big Data insights. Exceeds expectations in strategic thinking and execution.
Proficient (80-89%)
Shows solid understanding of customer intelligence and analytics. Applies concepts correctly with good analytical reasoning and practical application.
Developing (70-79%)
Displays basic competency in marketing concepts. Communication and analysis show understanding but lack depth or sophistication.
Beginning (60-69%)
Shows minimal understanding of course concepts. Work meets basic requirements but demonstrates limited application of learning outcomes.

Assessment-Specific Criteria:

Analytical Assessments (Case Analysis, Simulation):
  • Quality of data interpretation and insights
  • Strategic reasoning and recommendation soundness
  • Integration of course concepts
  • Feasibility and practical application
Communication Assessments (Presentations, Portfolio):
  • Clarity and organization of ideas
  • Professional presentation standards
  • Synthesis of complex information
  • Stakeholder-appropriate messaging
